References Aaron Swartz case and contrasts treatment of individual vs corporate information access. AI companies ingest copyrighted material at industrial scale. Anthropic settlement valued infringement at roughly $3,000 per book across 500,000 works, avoiding over $1 trillion in liability costs. When public knowledge becomes absorbed into proprietary, unauditable systems, information access shifts from democratic governance to corporate decision-making. Knowledge treatment represents a test of democratic commitments.
