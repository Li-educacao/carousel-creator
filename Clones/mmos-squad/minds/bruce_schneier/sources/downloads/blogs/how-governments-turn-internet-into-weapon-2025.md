296 deliberate internet shutdowns in 54 countries in 2024, and at least 244 more in 2025. The frequency has surged since Egypt's 2011 shutdown. India leads globally with 855 documented incidents. Internet shutdowns vary from total blackouts to targeted platform blocks. A nation's ability to enforce shutdowns depends on infrastructure centralization. The internet now constitutes essential infrastructure and denying access violates human rights. Reversing this trend requires governments to enshrine information access rights in law and practice.
