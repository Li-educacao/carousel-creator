# How I Optimised My Life to Make My Job Redundant
**Date:** July 17, 2015
**Source:** https://www.troyhunt.com/how-i-optimised-my-life-to-make-my-job/
**Category:** Personal & Reflective

Troy Hunt describes how he transitioned from full-time corporate employment to independent work through strategic optimization and deliberate effort.

## Key Strategies

### Multitasking and Context Switching

Hunt regularly juggled multiple projects simultaneously—blogging, speaking engagements, Pluralsight courses, and his primary job. He switched between tasks based on mood and energy levels, though he maintained focus during tasks requiring concentration like course recording.

### Optimized Work Environments

He invested substantially in productivity infrastructure, including high-quality ergonomic furniture and multiple monitors. Hunt also upgraded to a powerful laptop for travel productivity, recognizing that business-class seating enabled work during flights.

### Understanding Time Value

Hunt calculated what his time was worth financially, using this metric to decide where to invest effort and when to spend money to "buy" time through services like housekeeping or premium travel.

### Continuous Optimization

Inspired by his racing background, Hunt applied continuous improvement methodology to daily life, analyzing routines to eliminate inefficiency and remove distractions.

### Multipurposing Content

His approach involved creating once and repurposing extensively. Blog posts became Pluralsight courses, workshops, and conference talks, multiplying returns on initial effort.

### Extended Work Hours

Hunt acknowledged working until 1 a.m. regularly, starting at 6 a.m., and working weekends—a significant sacrifice enabling his achievements.

### Health Maintenance

He emphasized exercise as essential for sustaining high workloads, noting that different activities enhanced focus differently.

### Spousal Support

Hunt credited his wife's support as fundamental, stressing the importance of shared vision and agreement on priorities with one's partner.

## Core Insight

Hunt concludes that only 15% of financial success derives from technical knowledge, while 85% comes from communication and interpersonal skills—areas many technical professionals underdevelop.
