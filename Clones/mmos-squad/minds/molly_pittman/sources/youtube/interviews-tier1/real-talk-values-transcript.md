## Part 3: The Paradox of Scale and Intimacy

**Host:** You've gone from training hundreds to training thousands. How do you maintain intimacy with your community at that scale?

**Molly:** This is actually one of the biggest tensions I wrestle with. I want to know people's names. I want to understand their specific struggles. But I also want to serve more people.

The answer is that I can't do it all myself. I've had to build a team that carries the values I care about and extends that service mindset.

But here's what I've learned: I can't be intimate with 10,000 people. That's just not realistic. So I've had to accept that and build systems that allow other people to have that intimate experience with our community.

**Host:** How do you make sure the culture doesn't shift as you scale?

**Molly:** Ruthlessly. It's like... the moment I sense that we're making a decision primarily because it makes more money, rather than because it serves our students, I pump the brakes.

**Host:** You say you're "obsessed with teaching." Where does that come from?

**Molly:** I think it comes from a deep belief that knowledge and skills shouldn't be gatekept. When I learned media buying, it felt like there was this exclusive club, and if you weren't in it, you couldn't access the information.

So when I figured it out, my first instinct was: I need to make this accessible. I need to teach it.

It's not about making money, though money is a byproduct. It's about democratizing expertise.

**Host:** Do you feel like you've achieved that?

**Molly:** In some ways, yes. We've trained over 10,000 people. Many of them have gone on to build successful agencies, courses, and businesses of their own.

But there's still SO much gatekeeping in digital marketing. So many "gurus" who sell overpriced courses with basic information. That drives me crazy.

My mission is: high quality education at an accessible price point. That's the north star.
