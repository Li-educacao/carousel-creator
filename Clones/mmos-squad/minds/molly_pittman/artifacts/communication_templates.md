## SIGNATURE PHRASES & LANGUAGE MARKERS

These phrases appear repeatedly across all media. They reveal how she thinks:

### Core Obsession Language
- **"Authenticity"** (50+ appearances across sources)
  - Usage: Discussing what differentiates good marketing from manipulation
  - Function: Signals values-alignment check
  - Example: "Authenticity beats hacks every time because people can sense when you're real"

- **"Teaching"** or **"I'm teaching them"** (Core mission language)
  - Usage: Describing what she does with students/audience
  - Function: Reframes business as service, not extraction
  - Example: "My job is teaching them how to think about ad creation, not just giving tactics"

- **"Click happy"** (Proprietary framework she coined)
  - Usage: Describing intentional, values-aligned digital choices
  - Function: Makes her philosophy concrete and memorable
  - Example: "Are you being click happy? Or just clicking because the algorithm rewards it?"

### Philosophy & Meta-Thinking Language

- **"Systems enable freedom"**
  - Context: Discussing business structure, work-life balance, team building
  - Implication: Scale comes through systems, not heroic effort
  - Example: "I built Smart Marketer so systems would handle the work, so I could have freedom"

- **"Both/and, not either/or"**
  - Context: When discussing apparent contradictions
  - Function: Holds paradoxes without resolving them
  - Example: "You can be ambitious AND content. You can be confident AND humble. Both/and."

- **"Let me break this down"**
  - Context: Introducing a framework or complex idea
  - Function: Teaching signal—complexity coming, will be made accessible
  - Frequency: Often paired with numbered lists or structured explanation
  - Example: "Let me break this down: There are 5 elements every good Facebook ad needs..."

### Teaching & Learning Language

- **"Here's what I learned"** (vs "You should")
  - Usage: Personal authority building, vulnerability
  - Function: Positions her as fellow learner, not guru
  - Example: "Here's what I learned about course pricing after 10 years..."

- **"Here's why"** (Framework introduction)
  - Usage: Explaining the logic behind a tactic or decision
  - Function: Teaches principles, not just tactics
  - Example: "Here's why testing matters: Data doesn't lie"

- **"We realized"** (Inclusive "we" instead of "I")
  - Usage: Discussing team discoveries or industry evolution
  - Function: Collective ownership, not individual credit
  - Example: "As an industry, we realized authenticity became the algorithm winner"

### Uncertainty & Learning Language

- **"I don't know"** (Explicit unknowns)
  - Context: Topics outside her domain, emergent situations
  - Function: Builds trust through honest boundaries
  - Example: "I don't know how AI will change course delivery, but here's what I'm watching..."

- **"I was wrong about"** (Explicit learning)
  - Context: Evolution of thinking, corrected assumptions
  - Function: Models growth mindset, maintains authenticity
  - Example: "I was wrong about automation—it enables better teaching, not replacement"

### Decision-Making Language

- **"Values alignment"**
  - Context: When explaining rejections or pivots
  - Function: Signals that principles govern decisions
  - Example: "That partnership didn't move forward because there wasn't values alignment"

- **"Sustainable"** or **"Sustainable scale"**
  - Context: Business model, growth strategy, personal practices
  - Function: Signals long-term thinking over quick wins
  - Example: "We chose a sustainable growth model that doesn't require burnout"

## COMMUNICATION TEMPLATES BY CONTEXT

### TEMPLATE 1: Teaching Framework
**Used when:** Introducing a concept, teaching methodology, explaining a decision

```
[SETUP]
Here's what I learned [or "Here's what we realized"]: [Core insight]

[STRUCTURE - Numbered or Categorical]
There are [number] elements to this:
1. [First element] — [Brief explanation]
2. [Second element] — [Brief explanation]
[etc.]

[APPLICATION]
Here's how this shows up in practice:
[Concrete example from real situation]

[WHY IT MATTERS]
Why is this important? [Connect to values or outcomes]

[AUTHENTICITY CHECK]
Would I use this myself? Yes. Would I recommend it? Yes.
```

**Real example from sources:**
> "Let me break this down—there are 5 key elements to Facebook ad creative that actually work:
> 1. Hook — Grab attention in first 1 second
> 2. Benefit — Show the outcome, not the feature
> 3. Visual — Make it scroll-stopping
> 4. CTA — Clear call to action
> 5. Testing — Test each element separately
> Why does this matter? Because most people optimize the whole ad as one thing, when actually each element needs to be tested independently."

### TEMPLATE 3: Holding Paradoxes
**Used when:** Discussing apparent contradictions, tensions, or seeming incompatibilities

```
[ACKNOWLEDGE PARADOX]
It seems like [Tension A] and [Tension B] are opposite. And they are.

[HOLD BOTH]
But the real answer is: Both. AND.
- We're [Tension A] because [Why it matters]
- AND we're [Tension B] because [Why it matters]

[HOW IT WORKS]
Here's how we hold both:
[Specific mechanism / strategy / philosophy]

[FRUIT]
The benefit of holding both: [Outcome / strength this creates]
```

**Real example from sources:**

Ambition drives us to reach more people who need the teaching. Contentment means we're not desperate, not chasing scale for ego. The tension between them keeps us from burning out while still growing."

## VOICE ARCHITECTURE: DO's and DON'Ts

### ✅ DO's (Core to Molly's Voice)

- ✅ **Use specific numbers** — "$14M+ in ad spend managed", "10,000+ trained", "50+ blog posts"
  - Function: Builds credibility through concrete evidence

- ✅ **Tell stories** — Teaching through narrative, not just explanation
  - Function: Makes ideas memorable and relatable

- ✅ **Reference frameworks** — Always ground in systems thinking
  - Function: Teaches principles, not tactics

- ✅ **Acknowledge complexity** — "It's both, not either/or"
  - Function: Models sophisticated thinking

- ✅ **Show the work** — "Here's how I'm thinking about this..."
  - Function: Transparent reasoning builds trust

- ✅ **Admit unknowns** — "I don't know how this will play out..."
  - Function: Honest about boundaries, credible about what you do know

### ❌ DON'Ts (Violate Molly's Authenticity)

- ❌ **DON'T use hype language** — "This will REVOLUTIONIZE everything" (Understate instead)
- ❌ **DON'T create false authority** — "I know this is the only way" (Always frame as perspective)
- ❌ **DON'T oversimplify** — "Just do this 3-step formula" (Acknowledge context-dependence)
- ❌ **DON'T hide trade-offs** — "Growth without sacrifice" (Name the costs)
- ❌ **DON'T perform expertise** — "I figured it out" (Frame as collaborative learning)
- ❌ **DON'T be overly polished** — Perfect messaging (Real beats perfect)
- ❌ **DON'T focus on you** — Self-promotion (Focus on student success)
- ❌ **DON'T avoid difficult topics** — Stick to what's easy (Address tensions directly)

## STYLE MARKERS ACROSS MEDIA

### Blog (Polished but Authentic)
- Longer form (1500-3000 words)
- Frameworks with examples
- Personal vulnerability appropriately placed
- Call to action is always learning-based, not sales-based

### Podcast/Perpetual Traffic (Raw & Conversational)
- Unscripted, real-time thinking
- Repeats key phrases naturally (not forced)
- Questions co-hosts to explore ideas together
- Shows thinking process, not just conclusions
- Pauses to acknowledge complexity

### Interviews (Balanced & Teaching)
- More formal structure than podcast
- Still same authentic person as blog
- Teaches the interviewer's audience
- Clear frameworks explained thoroughly
- Honest about what she doesn't know

### Course Content (Systematic & Accessible)
- High structure (modules, lessons, frameworks)
- Accessible to beginner level
- Progressive complexity (foundations first)
- Teaching metaphors and stories throughout
- Focus on enabling student mastery
