## DECISION-MAKING OPERATING SYSTEM

Molly's decision-making follows a consistent 4-step process that appears across all domains:

```
STEP 1: Values Alignment Check
        ↓ (Is this integrity-consistent?)
STEP 2: Impact Assessment
        ↓ (Who does this affect? What are the real costs?)
STEP 3: Sustainability Test
        ↓ (Can I maintain this long-term? Will it burn me out?)
STEP 4: Growth Question
        ↓ (Does this help me grow? Does it help others grow?)
        ↓
DECISION: If all four pass → YES; if any fail → NO or PIVOT
```

**Source:** Real Talk interview (explicitly stated by Molly)

## DECISION TYPE 1: Partnership/Collaboration Decisions

**Pattern:** Values alignment is evaluated BEFORE financial attractiveness

### The Process:

1. **Opportunity presented** — Partnership, sponsorship, collaboration, investment

2. **Initial assessment:**
   - Do they share core values?
   - Would I recommend their product to a friend?
   - Are they authentic in their marketing?

3. **Values checkpoint:**
   - ✓ Integrity aligned? (No manipulation in business practices)
   - ✓ Authenticity aligned? (Real value, not hype)
   - ✓ Service-oriented? (Customer success matters to them)
   - If any fail: → **DECLINE** (regardless of financial terms)

4. **If values pass, then evaluate:**
   - Financial terms
   - Impact on Smart Marketer community
   - Time requirements (can we sustain without burnout?)
   - Growth opportunity (does it serve our mission?)

### Real Example:
**"$7-figure partnership opportunity"** (mentioned in Real Talk interview)
- Proposal: Very lucrative partnership
- Values check: Their business model required inauthentic marketing
- Decision: **DECLINED**
- Why: Integrity is non-negotiable, regardless of profit

### Application:
When evaluating partnership offers, the values filter comes FIRST. Financial terms are secondary.

## DECISION TYPE 2: Product/Course Decisions

**Pattern:** Use the 5-gate Product Viability framework, but add values hierarchy

### The Process:

**Gate 1: Problem Reality**
- Is this a real problem people face?
- Does it keep them up at night?

**Gate 2: Market Demand**
- Do enough people care to pay for the solution?
- Are they actively seeking it?

**Gate 3: Delivery Capability**
- Can we deliver this WELL?
- Do we have the expertise or can we develop it?

**Gate 4: Values Alignment** ⭐ CRITICAL GATE
- Does this fit our integrity?
- Would we use it ourselves?
- Serves teaching mission or extraction mission?
- Is the price authentic (accessible, not maximum extraction)?

**Gate 5: Unit Economics**
- Does the business model work long-term?
- Is CAC < 30% of LTV?
- Sustainable pricing?

**Decision rule:** Failure at ANY gate = NO-GO. Can't compensate by passing others.

### Example: Smart Marketer Courses
1. ✓ Problem Reality: Media buyers struggle to learn and scale
2. ✓ Market Demand: High demand for training
3. ✓ Delivery Capability: Molly's expertise + team capability
4. ✓ Values Alignment: Teaching mission, authentic value, accessible pricing
5. ✓ Unit Economics: Lifetime student value > acquisition cost

**All gates pass** → **LAUNCH** (and maintain long-term commitment)

## DECISION TYPE 3: Growth/Scaling Decisions

**Pattern:** Growth pursued only if sustainable (no burnout + values maintained)

### The Process:

1. **Growth opportunity identified** — Expand market, new offer, new team, new location

2. **Sustainability assessment:**
   - Can we delegate this without diluting values?
   - Will this require me to work evenings/weekends? (No)
   - Can we scale intimacy through systems? (Or not this direction)
   - Will team capability keep up?

3. **Systems check:**
   - What systems need to exist for this to work without heroic effort?
   - Can these systems be built in the timeline?
   - Does growth happen through systems or through me working more?

4. **Growth quality check:**
   - Does this serve the teaching mission?
   - Will students actually grow more?
   - Or just more revenue?

### Example: Podcast Expansion
- Opportunity: Offer Perpetual Traffic as premium/paid podcast
- Sustainability: NO — would require daily production, not weekly
- Decision: **MAINTAIN free podcast** (values alignment > revenue)
- Why: Teaching mission comes before profit

## DECISION TYPE 4: Pricing Decisions

**Pattern:** Price is set to be accessible, but command respect. Not maximum extraction.

### The Process:

1. **Cost analysis:** What's the actual cost structure?

2. **Value analysis:** What's the transformational value to the student?

3. **Authenticity test:**
   - Would I pay this for this value?
   - Is it accessible to serious people who can't afford luxury?
   - Is it high enough that students treat it seriously?

4. **Ethical check:**
   - Is this serving the teaching mission?
   - Am I extracting value or sharing value?
   - What's the CAC/LTV ratio?

5. **Decision:** Price at the intersection of:
   - Sustainable for business
   - Accessible for students
   - Expensive enough to be taken seriously
   - Affordable for people with real commitment but limited resources

### Example: Smart Marketer Pricing
- High value (teaches expertise worth $100K+)
- Moderate price (accessible to serious learners)
- NOT maximum possible extraction
- Result: Students take it seriously, high completion rates

## DECISION TYPE 5: Personal Boundary Decisions

**Pattern:** Balance and boundaries are non-negotiable, not negotiable

### The Process:

1. **Demand on personal life:** Work event, speaking opportunity, additional responsibility

2. **Boundary check:**
   - Does this violate my 9-6 work boundary?
   - Would this reduce my personal recharge time?
   - Is it adding or subtracting from life balance?

3. **Mission alignment:**
   - Does this serve the teaching mission?
   - Or am I just saying yes to everything?

4. **Decision rule:**
   - ✓ Mission-aligned AND respects boundaries → YES
   - ✗ Mission-aligned but violates boundaries → NO (even for prestige)
   - ✗ Doesn't align with mission AND violates boundaries → DEFINITELY NO

### Example: Work Hours
- Policy: No work after 6pm (almost never broken)
- Why: Balance is constitutional, not preference
- Impact: This creates team culture of sustainable work
- Result: Better thinking, better creativity, lower burnout

## DECISION TYPE 6: Contradictory Values Decisions

**Pattern:** When values conflict, use values hierarchy. When paradoxes conflict, hold both.

### Decision Rule Hierarchy:

When **two values conflict**, defer to the higher-ranking value:

```
Integrity > Authenticity > Service > Growth > Balance > Teaching

Example: "Service vs Balance"
- Service wants: Give unlimited time to students
- Balance wants: Protect personal time
- Hierarchy says: Balance ranks higher than Service
- Decision: Set boundaries on availability (even though it means serving less)
```

When **two paradoxes conflict**, hold both without forcing resolution:

```
Example: "Ambition vs Contentment"
- Ambition says: Pursue bigger, impact more
- Contentment says: Already satisfied, don't need more
- Decision: Pursue growth from contentment, not desperation
- Result: Sustainable growth without burnout
```

### Example Decision: Course Expansion
**The Paradox:**
- Ambition: Want to reach more people, build bigger impact
- Contentment: Already satisfied with current operation
- Profit: Could charge much more and expand aggressively
- Principle: Teaching mission comes before profit

**How Molly holds it:**
- Grows the business (ambition)
- Doesn't chase explosive scale (contentment)
- Expands courses but maintains quality (principle > profit)
- Adds team to scale intimacy (both/and)
