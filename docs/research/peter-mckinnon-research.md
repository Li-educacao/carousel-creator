## Creative Philosophy & Teaching Methodology

### Core Philosophy

Peter McKinnon's approach to content creation can be distilled into several interconnected principles:

**1. Editing as Emotional Architecture**
> "Video editing should support tone, not just structure."

McKinnon's cuts are never rushed. Transitions are purposeful, not flashy for the sake of being flashy. B-roll adds emotional weight, creating a sense of place, texture, and rhythm. His videos feel "less like a YouTube video and more like a short film with a little lesson hidden inside it."

**2. Experience Over Delivery**
> "Viewers don't remember every lens or setting recommended, but they remember how the video made them feel."

He believes editing is the chance to shape that feeling. Every technical tutorial is wrapped in storytelling that makes the viewer feel something beyond the information conveyed.

**3. Imperfect Action Over Perfect Inaction**
> "If you don't fail, you are not doing it right! If nobody ever made mistakes, life would be really boring!"

His content philosophy emphasizes that imperfect content posted consistently beats chasing perfection and posting nothing.

**4. Passion First, Everything Else Follows**
> "By taking the passion and putting it first, everything else follows."

This principle guided his transition from wedding photography to YouTube, from magic to filmmaking, and from content creation to product design.

### Signature Techniques

| Technique | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Speed ramping | Dynamic pacing in B-roll and transitions |
| Whip pans | Energetic scene transitions |
| Custom LUTs | Consistent cinematic color grading across projects |
| High Dynamic Range color correction | Rich, film-like image quality |
| Soft-focus B-roll | Documentary-feel atmospheric shots |
| Layered storytelling | Technical instruction wrapped in personal narrative |
| Emotional pacing | Slow builds that draw viewers in gradually |

### Teaching Approach

McKinnon's teaching methodology is characterized by:

1. **Accessibility first** -- Complex photography and filmmaking concepts broken down for beginners without condescension
2. **Show, don't just tell** -- Every tutorial features real-world demonstrations, not just screen recordings
3. **Personal vulnerability** -- Shares failures, burnout, creative blocks alongside successes
4. **Hollywood-grade production on YouTube** -- Behind-the-scenes of creative burnout and real creative struggles, proving the medium can be elevated
5. **Community building** -- Viewers become "superfans" because they feel they are learning alongside him, not below him

### On Social Media Evolution (2025)

McKinnon's 2025 stance on Instagram represents a mature philosophical position:
- Instagram is no longer a photo platform -- it is video-based
- Photographers need to create behind-the-scenes videos and personal stories, not just final products
- "We are given all the tools to make it work" -- embrace platform changes rather than resist them
- Social media is NOT your portfolio
