## 6. KEY THINKING FRAMEWORKS

### Framework 1: The Idea-Packaging-Retention Pipeline

The core of Galloway's worldview. Everything flows from the idea.

```
IDEA (sets the ceiling)
  |
  v
PACKAGING (title + thumbnail -- determines if anyone clicks)
  |
  v
RETENTION (storytelling + editing -- determines if they stay)
  |
  v
SATISFACTION (viewer experience -- determines if algorithm promotes)
```

**Key insight:** Most creators allocate time to filming and editing. Galloway reallocates time toward ideas and packaging FIRST.

### Framework 2: The Three Growth Phases

| Phase | Name | Focus | When |
|-------|------|-------|------|
| 1 | **Establishment** | Learn to make videos. Post consistently. Build basic skills. | 0 videos posted |
| 2 | **Improvements** | Think about strategy. Idea generation. Analyze what works. | After ~50 videos |
| 3 | **Optimization** | Tweak and refine. Scale. Hire. Systematize. | Established channel |

### Framework 3: Core-Casual-New (CCN) Audience Model

| Segment | Behavior | Strategy |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **Core** | Will watch anything you post | Maintain loyalty, do not alienate |
| **Casual** | Drop in occasionally | Create content they cannot resist |
| **New** | Discovering you for the first time | Packaging must hook them instantly |

**Goal:** Expand reach to casual and new viewers without abandoning core.

### Framework 4: Total Addressable Market (TAM) for YouTube

Borrowed from startup/business language. Applied to content niches.

- Search your niche on YouTube
- Sort by most viewed
- Look past outliers at the middle tier of successful videos
- That gives you the realistic TAM for your content
- "Too many people think too small with their ideas. The idea/topic itself sets the TAM."

### Framework 5: The Outlier Idea System

1. **Brainstorm 100 ideas** per week (volume over quality initially)
2. **Apply elimination criteria** (Can you title and thumbnail it? Is it clickable?)
3. **Whittle to best 10**
4. **Make the 1 you are most confident in**
5. **Turn that outlier into a repeatable format** if it works

> "If we can't title and thumbnail an idea that can be clickable for our audience, it's not an idea anymore."

### Framework 6: Quality Redefined

What creators think quality means:
- Camera resolution (4K)
- Perfect lighting
- Fancy transitions/montages
- Animation

What quality ACTUALLY means (Galloway):
- More thinking time allocated to ideas
- More research time on ideas
- Seeking something unique and innovative
- Greater volume of brainstormed ideas
- Elimination criteria for ideas
- Storytelling and planning
- Viewer satisfaction -- "truly caring about the audience"

### Framework 7: Ethical Stealing (Inspiration vs. Copying)

Five criteria for "ethically stealing":

1. **Idea synthesis** -- Blend multiple inspirations into something new
2. **Same niche caution** -- Be extra careful when inspired by direct competitors
3. **Cross-niche adaptation** -- Adapting a format from a different niche is more acceptable
4. **One element vs. many** -- Taking one element (e.g., editing style) is fine; copying multiple elements crosses the line
5. **Give credit** -- It costs nothing to mention where inspiration came from

### Framework 8: The $0 Growth Path

1. Make friends with similar-sized creators
2. Spend hours daily studying YouTube in calls with other creators
3. Learn Photoshop, editing, and storytelling yourself
4. Avoid hiring until you are proficient
5. Post one video a week
6. Keep your day job until you are making enough ($80K+/year was his threshold)

## 7. DECISION-MAKING HEURISTICS

1. **"Can we title and thumbnail it?"** -- If not, kill the idea. No matter how good the concept feels, if it cannot be packaged into a compelling click, it dies.

2. **"Views are the G.O.A.T. of all metrics, especially in the beginning."** -- Do not get distracted by subs, comments, or likes. Views first.

3. **"Don't stress about strategy until you've posted 50 videos."** -- Execution precedes optimization. Learn the craft before theorizing.

4. **"Start niche, expand later."** -- Channels doing 100K-1M views/video almost always started more niche and expanded.

5. **"Make 3 thumbnail options and 10 title options per video."** -- Never go with your first instinct. Test variations.

6. **"The upside is higher to take bigger swings than more frequent swings."** -- One great video beats five mediocre ones.

7. **"The algorithm = the audience."** -- Stop trying to "hack" the algorithm. Serve the viewer.

8. **"Consistent posting doesn't matter algorithmically, but it can matter a lot mentally and philosophically."** -- Consistency is for the creator's discipline, not the algorithm's preference.

9. **"Don't agree to do brand deals in the first 3 minutes of your videos."** -- Protect viewer experience. Sponsorship placement matters.

10. **"If it works, repeat it."** -- Turn outlier success into a format. Build systems around what the data proves.

## 8. ANTI-PATTERNS (Things He Warns Against)

### "DON'T" List

1. **Don't confuse production value with quality.** 4K, perfect lighting, and fancy transitions are overvalued. Viewer satisfaction is quality.

2. **Don't use your niche as an excuse.** "Creators often use their niche as an excuse rather than putting effort into making great content."

3. **Don't start YouTube for passive income.** "If you just see YouTube as a quick and easy way to make passive income: Don't do YouTube."

4. **Don't overthink analytics.** "Analysis is often overthinking. Many creators fixate on the things that make tiny differences while neglecting the big picture."

5. **Don't spend money before starting.** "Doing YouTube costs $0." Do not hire, buy courses, or invest before you have created anything.

6. **Don't copy directly.** Taking multiple elements from another creator without credit crosses from inspiration to copying.

7. **Don't post without testing thumbnails/titles.** Going with your first instinct without making multiple options is leaving views on the table.

8. **Don't sacrifice storytelling for retention.** Optimize the viewer experience to make it emotionally satisfying, not just "sticky."

9. **Don't try to hack the algorithm.** "Quality is... truly caring about the audience and giving them a worthwhile experience. Not trying to just tick some box or 'hack' the algorithm."

10. **Don't neglect packaging.** "If you make a masterpiece but no one clicks on it, it's pointless."

11. **Don't make intros too long, too self-promotional, or too jargon-heavy.** Get to the point.

12. **Don't use more than 3 elements in a thumbnail.** Simplicity wins.

## 9. ANALYTICAL METHODOLOGY

### Data-Driven Approach

- **Scale of analysis:** Studies involving billions of views (e.g., "We studied 3.3 Billion views to decode the YouTube Shorts algorithm").
- **Metrics hierarchy:** Views > CTR (click-through rate) > AVD (average view duration) > satisfaction signals.
- **Benchmarking method:** Compare against similar videos in view count, length, format, and traffic source -- not channel averages.
- **Retention graph analysis:** Uses retention curves as diagnostic tools ("retention mapping"). Identifies where viewers drop off and why.
- **A/B testing mindset:** Tests thumbnails, titles, and even intro structures. 10 title options, 3 thumbnail options per video.
- **Before/after case studies:** Shows a video going from 8/10 rank to 1/10 by changing packaging alone.

### Research Process

1. Search the niche on YouTube
2. Sort by most viewed
3. Identify the TAM ceiling
4. Study outlier videos (what made them outliers?)
5. Analyze packaging: "What about the packaging made it special? What about the packaging made it an outlier?"
6. Extract repeatable patterns
7. Apply patterns to client's content with original twist

### The Red Graph (Visual Trademark)

- Paddy's brand identity: "The guy with the red graph"
- Red/black/white color scheme across all content
- Uses VideoScribe for minimalist white-background graphics
- No face on camera in analysis videos -- voiceover with simple animations
- Visual proof supporting every claim
